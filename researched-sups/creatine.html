Creatine

Creatine Monohydrate is one of the most highly researched supplements in the fitness industry. Creatine is mostly supplemented by athletes, bodybuilders, and everyday gym-goers with the goal to enhance the ability to gain lean body mass.

Pros

  • May improve cognitive brain function (1)
  • May increase lean muscle mass (2)
  • May increase muscle strength (3)

Cons

  • May cause bloading temporarily (4)
  • Frequent vists to the bathroom

Common Questions

How much water should I be drinking when taking creatine?

Per Serving: It's recommended to drink about 12-17 ounces of water along with a typical 3-5 gram serving of creatine (5)

Daily Total: It's recommended to drink about 102 - 136 ounces of water per day while actively taking creatine. This helps manage the increase in water retention within your muscles due to creatine and supports overall hydration (6)

Do I Need "Load" Creatine?

It's not necessary to load creatine. The "loading" phase involves taking a high dose—typically 15-20 grams per day for 5-7 days—to quickly saturate the muscles with creatine. This is followed by a reduced daily maintenance dose of about 5 grams. Although loading can help you experience the effects of creatine sooner, it's not essential.

You can simply take 5 grams daily from the start, which will yield the same benefits over a longer period without the potential for side effects like stomach discomfort or increased water retention that can accompany the loading phase.

Who Should Not Supplement Creatine?

Creatine is widely used and considered safe for most people, but there are some individuals who should avoid it or consult with a healthcare provider before starting supplementation:

People with Kidney Disease: Those with pre-existing kidney conditions or reduced kidney function should be cautious, as creatine can potentially increase the workload on the kidneys.

People with Liver Disease: Since creatine is metabolized by the liver, those with liver conditions should also consult their doctor before using creatine supplements.

Diabetics: Creatine may affect blood sugar levels, so individuals with diabetes should monitor their blood sugar closely if they choose to use creatine.
